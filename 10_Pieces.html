Definition


I think that family for a Christian is your brothers and sisters in Christ. The people that a Christian is the closest to should be their fellow brethren in Jesus Christ. A believing family is a two-fold blessing as you can be close to them in relation and in Christ. It is no greater blessing than to be part of a Christian family with two loving parents and even siblings. In a sermon series about a year ago, Pastor John Street in his fellowship group, Joint Heirs gave five purposes for the family: [The family is the] Antidote to loneliness, The husband and wife is the primary relationship, [the family is] a place where people are to be honored, [the family is] a classroom where the most important lessons in life are taught and learned, and [the family is] a place of refuge in times of stormy weather in life (Street). Having a Christian family is a blessing in that you have a family that you can be closest to and you are also the same in wanting to please and honor Christ. Family is one of the most important institutions.

Literacy Narrative


When I was in first grade and going to Legacy Christian academy's homeschool program, I remember I loved to read, although I was just learning. I read everything that I could get my hands on. Around the end of first grade, I could read even what the "Big" third graders read. My mom was a patient and kind teacher. Since I was her only school-age child at the time, she could spend hours slowly teaching me the fundamentals of reading. Soon I was reading on my own, always books meant for several grades above my own. In third grade, I finished all of the books in the elementary library at Legacy. I love to read because I get to meet new characters who's lives are always full of adventure, wonders, unknowns, and almost always have a happy ending. People in my life have told me to slow down in reading because they think I will enjoy it more, but the faster I go, the more it feels like real-time talking in my head. I can read about one hundred pages an hour of a fiction or "fluff" book, as my dad calls it. I have read thousands of books and am now running out. Sometimes it takes me more time to research and find a good book than reading it. Recently due to running out of fiction books, as far as I know, I have moved to non-fiction. Non-fiction has a bit of a different allure. In Non-fiction, you don't meet magical characters living charmed lives that always turn out for the better. Non-fiction is about real people's hardships, discoveries, or the most crucial person in our lives, Jesus.

Memoir


I haven't had a whole lot of pivotal points in my life. One of my best memories is going to a Dodger game with my grandfather for my birthday. I had so much fun. The hot dogs were massive, and it was the best thing I had ever tasted. Our seats were great, and it was the largest place I had ever been to. Another great memory I have is when my robotics team was in the top one hundred in the United States that year (FLL). It felt great when my team got so far. I did much of the work as I was the only programmer. My most recent significant accomplishment was getting the project of the month on Juni Learning when I programmed my Discord Bot in the Python programming language.

The most pivotal point in my life, I think, was when my grandfather died in December of 2014; I had never been so sad in my entire life and still haven't since then. I saw my grandfather every week when we went to church with them for as long as I could remember. Before going to my grandfather's funeral and memorial, I had never been to one; it was the first time that someone I even knew died. The most pivotal point in my life was my grandfather's death, and I hope that I will see him again in heaven one day. My family still takes my grandmother to church every Sunday.
