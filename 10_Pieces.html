Literacy Narrative


When I was in first grade and going to Legacy Christian academy's homeschool program, I remember I loved to read, although I was just learning. I read everything that I could get my hands on. Around the end of first grade, I could read even what the "Big" third graders read. My mom was a patient and kind teacher. Since I was her only school-age child at the time, she could spend hours slowly teaching me the fundamentals of reading. Soon I was reading on my own, always books meant for several grades above my own. In third grade, I finished all of the books in the elementary library at Legacy. I love to read because I get to meet new characters who's lives are always full of adventure, wonders, unknowns, and almost always have a happy ending. People in my life have told me to slow down in reading because they think I will enjoy it more, but the faster I go, the more it feels like real-time talking in my head. I can read about one hundred pages an hour of a fiction or "fluff" book, as my dad calls it. I have read thousands of books and am now running out. Sometimes it takes me more time to research and find a good book than reading it. Recently due to running out of fiction books, as far as I know, I have moved to non-fiction. Non-fiction has a bit of a different allure. In Non-fiction, you don't meet magical characters living charmed lives that always turn out for the better. Non-fiction is about real people's hardships, discoveries, or the most crucial person in our lives, Jesus.

Process/Analysis: Creating a Minecraft Server


To create a Minecraft server, you must follow these simple steps carefully. Step one, download required resources from the Java website, download Java 8, and download the Minecraft server Jar file from the Minecraft website. Step two, setup install Java 8, create a folder to store your Minecraft server, and put the Minecraft Server Jar in the folder. Step three, EULA double click on the Minecraft server Jar in your Minecraft server folder, wait until the Jar opens then closes again. There should be several new files, one of which should be called EULA.txt, open EULA.txt, and change EULA=false to EULA=true. Step four, generation double click the Minecraft server Jar file again, now you must wait for Minecraft to download and install all of the files it depends on and generate your Minecraft world. Step Five, finding IP to join your Minecraft server, you must find out your IP. To do so, open cmd and type in "ipconfig" then press enter, look for a number that starts with "192.168." write down the whole number. Step six Joining now, anyone on your internet may open Minecraft, go to Multiplayer, and type in the number you wrote down in step five and press join. Congratulations, you just fully set up the most basic type of Minecraft server.

There are many other things that you can do, including adding modifications to your server, more commonly known as Mods. To do this, instead of downloading the Minecraft Server Jar from the Minecraft website, you download the installer from the Minecraft Forge website. Open the Forge installer and install both the server in the folder that you created and on the client version of Minecraft, the client version is the one you play on. Instead of opening the "minecraft_server.jar," you open the "forge-VERSION.jar." Forge will create a folder called Mods in the Mods folder, and you can put all of your modifications in that folder. Close the Forge jar. You can download any mods that you want from the Curseforge website. You must copy the mods to both the server folder and the client folder. You can get to the client folder by first opening the File Explorer and type in %appdata% into the address bar. Open the ".Minecraft" folder and create a folder called "mods" if it doesn't already exist. You can now open the Forge jar again. Now open Minecraft and click the dropdown in the bottom left corner and select forge. Now click play and then follow the same steps for joining.
